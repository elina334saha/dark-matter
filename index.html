Dark Matter

The Invisible Glue of the Universe

What is Dark Matter?

Dark matter is a mysterious form of matter that does not emit, absorb, or reflect lightâ€”making it invisible to current instruments. Yet, its presence is revealed through gravitational effects on visible matter, such as galaxies and galaxy clusters.

Why Does it Matter?

Without dark matter, galaxies would fly apart. Scientists estimate it makes up about 27% of the universe. Regular matter, the stuff we see, makes up just 5%.
